# The Historical Atlas of the Indian Subcontinent
### Map Series Roadmap — toward an encyclopaedia of kingdoms

*Compiled as a production plan for a series of plates in the style of*
**“South Asia in the Age of the Pālas, c. 750–1150 CE.”**

---

## How the series works

Each **plate** is not a single kingdom but an **era**, anchored on one paramount or
defining power and showing its contemporaries as a *composite* of spheres of influence —
exactly as the Pāla plate shows Pratihāra, Rāṣṭrakūṭa, Chola and the rest around the Pāla
core. This keeps every map self-contained yet comparable across the encyclopaedia.

The list below runs **backwards from the Pālas** (the path you asked for), then offers a
**forward volume** and a **southern parallel track** so the finished atlas covers the whole
subcontinent without gaps.

**Status key:** ✅ done · ◻ planned

## VOLUME I — Backwards from the Pālas
*Reverse-chronological. Start here and work “up” the timeline.*

### Plate 1 — The Age of the Pālas ✅
- **Span:** c. 750 – 1150 CE
- **Anchor:** Pāla Empire of Bengal & Bihar
- **Show alongside:** Gurjara-Pratihāra, Rāṣṭrakūṭa, Chola, Chālukya, Kalacuri, and the regional houses
- **Notable for:** the tripartite struggle for Kanauj; last great Buddhist patronage
- *Completed — the reference plate for the whole series.*

### Plate 2 — The Age of Harsha ◻
- **Span:** c. 600 – 650 CE
- **Anchor:** Vardhana (Pushyabhūti) Empire of Harsha, from Thānesar & Kanauj
- **Show alongside:** Later Guptas, Maukharis, Maitrakas of Valabhī, Chālukyas of Bādāmi (Pulakeśin II), Pallavas, Gauḍa under Śaśāṅka, Kāmarūpa
- **Notable for:** the last great northern empire before the early-medieval fragmentation; Xuanzang’s travels
- **Suggested title:** *North India in the Age of Harshavardhana*

### Plate 3 — The Gupta Golden Age ◻
- **Span:** c. 320 – 550 CE
- **Anchor:** Gupta Empire (Chandragupta I → Skandagupta)
- **Show alongside:** Vākāṭakas of the Deccan, Western Kshatrapas, Pallavas, Kadambas, Vishṇukuṇḍins, the Hūṇa incursions
- **Notable for:** the “classical” age of Sanskrit literature, science and temple art
- **Suggested title:** *India in the Gupta Golden Age*

### Plate 4 — The Kushan Empire ◻
- **Span:** c. 30 – 375 CE (peak under Kanishka, c. 127 CE)
- **Anchor:** Kushan Empire (Bactria to the Gangetic plain)
- **Show alongside:** Western Kshatrapas, Satavāhanas, the early Pallavas, Indo-Parthians
- **Notable for:** Silk-Road trade, Gandhāra & Mathurā art, Buddhism’s spread into Central Asia
- **Suggested title:** *The Crossroads Empire — India under the Kushans*

### Plate 5 — The Satavāhana Age ◻
- **Span:** c. 100 BCE – 220 CE *(dynastic origin debated, possibly 2nd c. BCE)*
- **Anchor:** Satavāhana Empire of the Deccan
- **Show alongside:** Western Kshatrapas (Sakas), Indo-Scythians, Shungas/Kanvas, the Tamil Sangam kingdoms
- **Notable for:** the first great Deccan power; the Saka–Satavāhana rivalry
- **Suggested title:** *The Deccan Bridge — the Satavāhana Age*

### Plate 6 — The Age of Invasions: Shungas, Greeks & Sakas ◻
- **Span:** c. 185 BCE – 50 CE
- **Anchor:** Shunga Empire of Magadha (and the Kanvas) — *or* the Indo-Greek kingdoms as co-anchor
- **Show alongside:** Indo-Greek kingdoms (Menander I), Indo-Scythians, Indo-Parthians, early Satavāhanas, Kaliṅga under Khāravela
- **Notable for:** the fragmentation after Mauryan collapse; Hellenistic contact
- **Suggested title:** *After the Mauryas — Shungas, Greeks and Sakas*

### Plate 7 — The Mauryan Empire ◻
- **Span:** c. 322 – 185 BCE (zenith under Aśoka, c. 268–232 BCE)
- **Anchor:** Maurya Empire, from Pāṭaliputra
- **Show alongside:** the Tamilakam kingdoms (Chera, Chola, Pāṇḍya), the Seleucid frontier, unconquered Kaliṅga (pre-261 BCE)
- **Notable for:** the first subcontinental empire; the Aśokan edicts and Dhamma
- **Suggested title:** *The First Empire — India under the Mauryas*

### Plate 8 — The Sixteen Mahājanapadas ◻
- **Span:** c. 600 – 345 BCE (into the Nanda ascendancy)
- **Anchor:** the *ṣoḍaśa mahājanapada* — the sixteen great realms
- **Show alongside:** the rise of Magadha (Haryanka & Nanda dynasties), Gandhāra under Achaemenid Persia
- **Notable for:** the political world of the Buddha and Mahāvīra; the seedbed of empire
- **Suggested title:** *The Sixteen Great Realms — the Mahājanapada Age*

### Plate 9 — The Later Vedic Janapadas ◻
- **Span:** c. 1100 – 600 BCE
- **Anchor:** the early *janapadas* — Kuru, Pañchāla, Kāśī, Kosala, Videha
- **Show alongside:** the Painted Grey Ware cultural zone; tribal polities of the Indo-Gangetic plain
- **Notable for:** the transition from pastoral lineages to territorial kingship
- **Suggested title:** *The Vedic Heartland — the Age of the Janapadas*

### Plate 10 — The Indus Civilisation ◻ *(civilisational, not dynastic)*
- **Span:** c. 3300 – 1300 BCE (Mature Harappan c. 2600–1900 BCE)
- **Anchor:** the Indus (Harappan) Civilisation
- **Show alongside:** major cities — Harappā, Mohenjo-daro, Dholāvīra, Rākhīgaṛhī, Lothal — and trade links to Mesopotamia
- **Notable for:** the subcontinent’s first urban order; treat as a *city-and-trade* plate rather than a kingdom plate
- **Suggested title:** *The First Cities — the Indus Civilisation*

## SOUTHERN PARALLEL TRACK
*The peninsula deserves its own plates, since northern eras under-represent it.*

- ◻ **The Tamil Sangam Age** — c. 300 BCE – 300 CE — Chera, Chola, Pāṇḍya of Tamilakam

- ◻ **The Chola Imperial Zenith** — c. 985 – 1150 CE — Rājarāja I & Rājendra I; naval reach to Śrīvijaya *(a reference image for this already sits in `map example resource/`)*

## Design notes for every plate
*To keep the encyclopaedia visually consistent.*

1. **Same frame:** parchment ground, gold double border, corner flourishes, compass rose, title cartouche, scale note.
2. **Core vs. sphere:** solid fill for settled core territory; diagonal hatching for the wider sphere of influence.
3. **One anchor power highlighted** in saffron-orange; contemporaries muted.
4. **Standard sections below the map:** Introduction · Timeline · Kingdoms in Detail · Powers Beyond the Frontier · Cultural Context · Method · Sources.
5. **Standard type:** Cinzel (display) · EB Garamond (body) · Cormorant Garamond (accents).
6. Every plate credited and dated; boundaries always flagged as approximate, composite spheres.
